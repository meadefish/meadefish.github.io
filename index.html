About Me

I am a Data Scientist at Santander UK, based in London developing data-driven applications using analytics and machine learning to automate and enhance internal audit functionality.

Originally from the West Midlands (UK), in 2010 I moved to London to pursue a 4-year (with industrial placement) BSc. (Hon) degree in Software Engineering at City University London. I then moved to Lancaster to pursue a MSc. degree in Data Science at Lancaster University before returning to begin my career as a Data Scientist in London.

During my time at City University London, I was awarded with the Best Undergraduate Thesis & Project award for my work on using Bayes Theorem and time-series analysis to predict the result of football matches across a Premier League season (see here) whilst working under the supervision of Dr. Peter Smith.

Before joining Santander UK, I worked as a Data Scientist at SAS where I gained exposure working in a client-facing environment, applying analytical and machine learning techniques to problems involving NLP, online advertisement placement and workforce recruitment optimisation. Prior to joining SAS, I worked as a Data Scientist (summer intern) at FWD Research applying predictive algorithms in the travel insurance domain and as a Data Scientist (intern) at LateRooms.com applying predictive algorithms to optimise hotel room bookings.
